FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions)

What are the requirements for Soliloquy?

Soliloquy requires at least WordPress 3.8 and a modern web browser. We do not support IE8 or below.

How do support and updates work for Soliloquy?

If you purchase the Master license, you get support and updates for the lifetime of the product. Any other license purchase gives you access to one year of updates and support. Emails will be sent prior to your license expiring with information on how to renew your license (including a renewal discount). The renewal process is not automatic, but you will lose access to updates and support if your license happens to expire. You can continue to use the plugin with an expired license, but no updates or support will be provided.

Is Soliloquy compatible with WordPress MultiSite?

Yes! Soliloquy works perfectly with WordPress MultiSite, both network activated and activated on individual sites. The automatic updates also work for MS as well.

Is Soliloquy translatable?

Yes, Soliloquy has full translation and localization support via the soliloquy textdomain. All .mo and .po translation files should go into the languages folder in the base of the plugin. The same is true for every Soliloquy Addon as well.

Can I upgrade my license after my initial purchase?

Sure! Upgrading Soliloquy is very easy - if your license has the ability to be upgraded, you can upgrade right from your account overview screen by either clicking on the Upgrade link in the header navigation or the Upgrade tab on the page. The table that appears on the page will show available upgrades, and the price difference for upgrading will already be calculated for you based on the license you select.

Do you offer refunds?

If you are not completely satisfied with Soliloquy after 14 days, you can request a refund and we will give you 100% of your money back - no questions asked.

Can I package Soliloquy to sell within a theme?

Sure. You must purchase the Master license to package Soliloquy within a theme.

Does Soliloquy work on non-WordPress sites or WordPress.com?

No. Soliloquy is a WordPress plugin, so it will not work on sites that do not use WordPress as its primary CMS. Additionally, Soliloquy is not compatible with the WordPress.com platform. You must be using a self-hosted version of WordPress to utilize Soliloquy on your website.
